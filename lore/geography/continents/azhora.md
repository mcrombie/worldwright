# Azhora

Azhora is the continent — the homeland of the civilization that produced these records, the land whose history fills the libraries and whose geography is known in more detail than any other place on Corav. It is a large continent occupying a temperate to subtropical band, running roughly north to south, with a climate range from cold maritime in the north to cold maritime again in the south for entirely different reasons, and an arid interior in the east that produces the only true desert on the known landmass.

The name "Azhora" is discussed at length elsewhere. It began as a valley name. It became a continent's name. These things happen.

## Shape and Orientation

Azhora runs approximately north to south, broadest in its central-northern portion and narrowing progressively toward the south, where the Bouén peninsula extends as a final arm into the open southern ocean. The western coast faces the open western sea; the eastern coast faces the Iberos Sea and its island chains. A central spine of hills and low mountains divides the continent's drainage, separating the westward-flowing river systems of Mittolo from the shorter, faster rivers that run east to the Iberos coast.

## Climate Zones

The continent's climate is determined by three overlapping systems: prevailing winds from the western sea, the rain shadow cast by the interior ridges, and the two ocean currents that define the northern and southern coasts.

**The North** is cold. The Bay of Lol and Smunders Bay are winter-cold, summer-mild, and wet throughout the year. The Ganoss uplands above are brutal in winter and briefly spectacular in summer. The peoples here are adapted — culturally, economically, and by all physiological appearances — to what the land demands.

**Western and Central Azhora (Mittolo)** is the temperate heartland. Reliable rain, long growing seasons, the great river systems providing both irrigation and transport. This is where the continent's agricultural wealth is concentrated and where its dominant political institutions grew.

**The Pyros regions** sit at the hinge between wet and dry. West Pyros catches the last of the western moisture; East Pyros sits in the rain shadow. The ridge that divides them is one of the most consequential geographic features on the continent.

**The Moroshé Desert and the Arid Interior** occupy the broad eastern interior south of the Pyros hinge. The interior is genuinely arid — desert in the full sense in its central sections. But the eastern coast, facing the warm Iberos Sea, is Mediterranean: warm, dry summers, mild wet winters, the kind of light that makes everything look edible.

**The South (Bouén)** is an anomaly. By latitude it should be warm. A cold ocean current — the Deep River — sweeps up from the southern polar waters along the western and southern coasts of the Bouén peninsula, dragging cold upwelling and persistent overcast with it. The result is cool, grey, fog-prone, and inhabited by people who have built a civilization that is specifically and ingeniously suited to exactly those conditions.

## The Regions

Azhora's major regions each have their own distinct geography, history, and culture:

- **Mittolo** — the forested central heartland, seat of the dominant civilization
- **Northern Azhora** — the cold coastal north, Bay of Lol and Smunders Bay, the Ganoss uplands, the Orched Hills
- **Pyros** — West Pyros (volcanic, fertile, contested) and East Pyros (the trade corridor, the rain shadow)
- **The Plains** — the great transitional grasslands, home to the continent's oldest peoples
- **Moroshé Desert** — the arid interior, Mediterranean coasts, ancient ruins, distinct cultures
- **Iberos Coast** — the eastern seaboard, the island chains, the Iberos Sea maritime world
- **Alezhor** — the gold coast, coastal strip between the western sea and the great southwestern forest, independent city-states
- **Legemum** — the western peninsula, tin country, no king, the miners' and fishers' guilds
- **Dinova** — the largest southern peninsula, the Meroshe desert interior, the Kingdom of Marosh, the commercial kingdom of Hama
- **Bouén** — the cold southern peninsula, the Deep River current, the Azner Shores

## What Lies Beyond

The coasts of Azhora face open ocean in three directions. To the west: the open western sea, the Silence, and the speculated Western Shore. To the northeast: the Outer Islands and, perhaps, Canon. To the south: the open southern ocean and whatever the Deep River comes from.

Azhoran civilization has not yet answered these questions. The records that will address them have not yet been written.
