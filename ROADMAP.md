### Layer 5 — History
The temporal world. Not yet documented.

The following historical events are referenced in existing lore and deserve their own documents eventually:

- The First Silence — pre-history of Mittolo; "the people before us left"

- The Pyrosi occupation resistance — the Pyrosi Method of outlasting occupiers; who occupied them
- The failed Azhoran trading post on Brocerri's leeward coast — what happened is not in official records
- The Bouéni western crossing attempts — reports of the Western Shore, still contested
- The two successful Academy northern expeditions — what they found, what they didn't find, why the third expedition is perpetually in planning

### Layer 7 — Mysteries
The things that don't have explanations yet — by design.

These are not missing documents. They are open questions that should remain open indefinitely, with evidence accumulating around them but no resolution. Each is already present in the lore as an unanswered question.

- **The western landmass** — appears in children's maps, refused by the Academy, hinted at by Bouéni western crossings. Never confirmed.
- **The Qadimuur** — four-consonant roots, verb-final order, words from nowhere. What the desert peoples say and what the linguist who studied it doesn't say.
- **The substrate origin** — Bouéni "we came from the sea"; the swell-navigation connection to the archipelago; the Blue Stones notation. Hypothesis exists; proof doesn't.
- **The Red Meridian carvings** — untranslated; coastal peoples say it "prevents something from happening again." What?
- **The worked seafloor in the Silence** — visible through unusually clear water. What was it? Why is there no wind above it?
- **The Ascending Peaks** — why does it affect stone and not water or metal? Is it natural?
- **The northern continent** — it doesn't want to be found. What does that mean exactly?
- **The stone enclosures on Cold Stones** — predate Azhoran presence; no known function; underfunded archaeology.
- **The ruins in the Moroshé** — built when the desert wasn't a desert; the desert peoples won't discuss why they don't use the materials.

## Two-Scale Game (Idea)

A design sketch for using the hex map as the foundation for a two-layer strategy game is in [`docs/two-scale-game.md`](docs/two-scale-game.md).

The core idea: regions are strategic nodes (Clashvergence-compatible), and when armies clash in a region, the game zooms into that region's hexes for a tactical battle. Terrain on the hexes drives movement costs, defense bonuses, and entry positions. The map editor already produces all the data the game would need — no extra authoring step required.

Key open decisions before implementation:
- Keep `gameState` separate from `MapData`, or embed it?
- Full hex tactics vs. terrain-modified dice rolls?
- Local-only, hotseat, or LAN multiplayer?
