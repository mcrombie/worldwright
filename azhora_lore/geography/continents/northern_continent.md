# The Northern Continent

The northern continent has no agreed Azhoran name. Scholars refer to it as the Northern Continent, the Cold Land, or simply the North, all of which are admissions of ignorance dressed as geography. The Azhorans know it exists. They know its southernmost feature — Ice Spear — from a distance. Beyond that, almost nothing is confirmed.

What Azhoran scholars have not yet established — though a careful reading of Elodi scripture may eventually force the question — is whether "the northern continent" describes a single landmass at all. The Elodi texts, which preserve detailed geographical memory of what lies east of Azhora, describe the world beyond as three distinct but connected continents: a northern arm, a southern arm, and an eastern body, joined at a central point the way Afroeurasia's three continents are joined on a planetary scale. By this account, what Azhorans call the northern continent is only the closest arm of something much larger — a supercontinent whose western edge is visible from Cold Stones, whose eastern extent no Azhoran navigator has reached, and at whose center, where all three arms converge, the Old Kingdom has stood since before any current civilization can remember.

Azhoran scholars who engage with Elodi texts tend to note this geographical description and move past it quickly. The implication — that the landmass Azhora faces is not a remote Arctic territory but the near edge of the world's oldest and largest civilization — is one that would require a great deal of existing cartographic work to reconsider.

What is known comes from three sources: the Cold Stones expeditions, which have never ventured beyond the archipelago in force; a small number of accounts from ships that claim to have reached Ice Spear's base and returned; and a body of oral tradition in the northern Azhoran coastal cultures, particularly around Smunders Bay, that treats the North as a source of legend rather than of navigable territory.

The oral tradition is the most detailed of the three sources and the least verifiable. It describes the northern continent as ancient beyond reckoning, inhabited by peoples or things that predate Azhoran civilization by an order of magnitude, and fundamentally different in character from the lands Azhorans know. The word used in the Bay of Lol tradition for the northern continent translates roughly as "the place that does not want to be found," which is not a navigational description but may be the most accurate one available.

## What Can Be Observed

From the highest points on Azhora's northern coast — certain headlands above Smunders Bay in clear weather, and more reliably from the northern edges of the Cold Stones archipelago — the southern coast of the northern continent is visible as a dark line on the horizon. On exceptional days, the shape of Ice Spear resolves: a long, pale peninsula extending southward into the sea between the continents, tapering to a sharp point, flanked by glacial cliffs that catch the low northern light.

It is large. The visible coastline extends beyond the horizon in both directions. The interior rises steeply — mountains are visible from Cold Stones, high and snow-capped, running east-west behind the Ice Spear peninsula. How deep the continent extends behind those mountains has not been established.

## Ice Spear

Ice Spear is the only part of the northern continent that Azhorans have approached closely enough to describe. See its own entry for details. It is the southernmost extension of a rugged, glaciated coastline that continues east and west into the unknown. What makes Ice Spear distinct from the rest of the coastline — why it extends so much further south, tapering to its characteristic point — is a question of geology that no Azhoran expedition has yet been in a position to investigate.

## Rumors and Tradition

The northern coastal peoples of Azhora — particularly the Ganoss upland clans, who are closest geographically and most exposed to whatever the North chooses to send southward — maintain traditions about the northern continent that they share selectively. Common elements across traditions: the continent is inhabited, and has been for longer than Azhora's own record. The inhabitants are not friendly or unfriendly — these are not the right categories. They are different in a way that makes interaction unpredictable in ways that have not historically gone well for the Azhoran side.

There are accounts, difficult to verify, of ships from the north arriving on Azhora's northern coast. Not recently. In the time before the Caeras Compact, possibly much earlier. What these ships carried, who was on them, and what happened afterward is not clear from any surviving record. The Ganoss traditions contain something that may be a reference to such a contact, but the tradition has been interpreted in enough different ways that certainty is not available.

What the Ganoss clans are consistent about: the North is not merely cold and distant. It is specifically the kind of unknown that warrants respect rather than simple caution. This distinction — between things that are dangerous because they are powerful and things that are other in a more fundamental sense — appears in their language as a grammatical category that standard Mittoli does not have a direct translation for.

## Research Status

The Azhoran Academy of Natural History has twice funded formal expeditions toward the northern continent. The first, sixty years ago, reached Cold Stones and returned with extensive notes on the archipelago but did not attempt the Ice Spear crossing. The second, twenty years ago, reached Cold Stones, spent one winter there, and sent a small advance party that came within sight of Ice Spear before weather forced a return.

A third expedition is in the planning stages. It has been in the planning stages for eight years, which says something about either the difficulty of the logistics or the collective appetite for what it might find.
