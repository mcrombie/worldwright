# The Narcoshi

The Narcoshi live in six connected valleys in the northern Oremindi — a cluster of parallel ridges between the Mithala plains and Cape Thalmagar to the north — and their life is organized around a breed of goat. Not metaphorically. The geography of their country is the geography the goats navigate; the routes between the six valleys pass through cliff faces and rock chimneys that human feet learned from goat feet, following paths that no outside observer would identify as paths because the goats found them first. To be Narcoshi is to know these routes, and the routes are inseparable from the goats.

They are the last inhabited people before the northern passes to Thalmagar. They know the Long-Back — the cold northern approach — from direct experience rather than from accounts. They are patient, difficult to impress, and capable of explaining the biology of their goats in considerable detail to outsiders who will, in most cases, continue to find the goats unsettling.

---

## The Goats

Three distinct breeds inhabit Narcosh, each developed for specific terrain in the six-valley system. The one that attracts the most outside attention — and the most misunderstanding — is the vel-ridge breed: near-black with pale facial markings that look, to unprepared observers, like symbols; swept-back heavily-ridged horns that curve in ways that trigger specific and unhelpful pattern-recognition; pale luminous eyes adapted to low-light environments. Each of these features has a practical explanation. The dark coloration absorbs heat in a cold-altitude environment. The pale facial markings create contrast that aids in identifying individuals in low light — useful for the goats' own social recognition and for the herders who monitor them. The horn shape allows passage through rock chimneys where a different curve would catch. The eye structure is altitude adaptation for winter light conditions.

Most outsiders do not reach this explanation. What they see is: black goats with pale symbol-like markings, strange horns, and luminous eyes, being given what appears to be ritualized attention by the people keeping them. The conclusion these observers generally reach — that the Narcoshi are engaged in some form of devil-goat worship — is wrong in every particular. The Narcoshi are not worshipping the goats. They are keeping the record. The elaborate ritual structures around goat care are practical knowledge encoded in traditional form: information about health, breeding, seasonal condition, route fitness, and the inter-valley passage geometry, maintained through practice structures that ensure the knowledge does not degrade across generations.

The Narcoshi response to the "Devil Goat Worshippers" slur is patient, patient, eventually patient contempt. They can explain the biology. They have explained the biology. They have found that outsiders who arrive at the devil-goat reading from one glance do not reliably revise it when given accurate information, and they have adjusted their expectations accordingly.

## The Vel-Narcoth

*Vel-narcoth* means "the ways the goats go." It refers to the inter-valley routes through cliff faces and rock chimneys that connect the six Narcoshi valleys — routes that are not visible as paths to outside observers and that the goats found before the people did. The goats navigate by routes that follow specific air and water current patterns through the rock. They are not visible from above. They are not visible from below in most conditions. They pass through spaces that appear, from outside, to be sheer cliff face.

The knowledge of the vel-narcoth — its complete geography, its seasonal variations (some chimneys are passable only in certain weather and for a certain window before ice makes them impassable), its condition in different years — is held by the route-keeper elder of the valley assembly. The route-keeper is the most authoritative figure in Narcoshi governance in the specific sense that their knowledge is what the country's geography actually is. Without it, the six valleys are six separate places. With it, they are one networked territory with internal mobility that no outside power has been able to map.

No outside power has successfully administered all six valleys simultaneously, because no single point of control accesses the full vel-narcoth network. Powers that have controlled one valley entrance have controlled one valley. The other five have continued as normal.

## Economy and Governance

The valley assembly meets twice yearly, in spring and autumn, when conditions allow inter-valley movement. The route-keeper presides over the assembly in the specific sense of having the most authoritative knowledge of the routes that make the assembly possible. The assembly arbitrates inter-valley disputes, manages the seasonal grazing arrangements, and sets the terms for whatever external trade is happening.

External trade consists primarily of two things: eastern-valley cheese and vel-ridge fiber. The cheese commands premium prices at Iberos coast markets in a way that Narcoshi elders describe as "flavor irreproducible outside conditions" — the specific botanical diet of the eastern valley, the water chemistry, the temperature cycling, and the breeds combine to produce a taste that outside imitation does not replicate. The Narcoshi do not export the method. They sell the cheese.

The fiber from the vel-ridge breed is the other significant export — dense, warm, water-resistant, with specific properties that highland-spinning traditions convert into textiles that lowland equivalents do not match. The fiber trade is more direct than the cheese trade: it moves by the same routes that bring grain imports into Narcosh in exchange.

The Narcoshi import grain because the valley floors, while habitable, are not large enough at altitude to support the population entirely. This is the only significant external dependency. It is managed through the valley assembly's collective purchasing arrangements, which ensures that no single outside supplier has leverage over the full valley system.

## Character

The Narcoshi are difficult to offend and difficult to impress. Both of these are the same trait expressed differently: a people whose primary knowledge is practical and technical, operating in terrain that enforces consequences for error, develop a uniform respect for demonstrated competence and a uniform indifference to claimed status or performance. An outside lord who arrives in Narcosh expecting the deference owed to their rank will find the deference owed to their rank and nothing else; the route-keeper elder will speak to them the same way they speak to everyone, which is to say directly.

Their humor is dry to the point of opacity — literal answers to questions whose import was figurative, extended with complete neutrality until the recipient either realizes the joke or doesn't. Both outcomes are acceptable. The Narcoshi are patient with the second outcome in the same way they are patient with everything else that takes time.

The valley communities have distinct characters within the overall Narcoshi culture — the eastern valleys, which have the most profitable trade and the most contact with the Iberos markets, are somewhat more externally oriented; the northern valleys, which abut the Long-Back territory and have the most direct knowledge of Thalmagar's megafauna zone, are the most closed. All six share the defining orientation: routes, goats, the vel-narcoth, and the valley assembly that connects them.

## Language

The Narcoshi speak a Mittoli-derived language with an extremely specific goat vocabulary — technical terminology for the breeds, their conditions, the pass geometry, the seasonal route variations — that functions as a closed vocabulary even for other Mittoli speakers. There is a pre-Mittoli substrate in the deep valley place names: the same layer visible in Mithala, Yunethre, Celder, and Lotharn, suggesting the Narcoshi valleys were occupied by the same older population whose geography-words survived the language transition.

## Summary of Key Points

- The Narcoshi are the people of six connected valleys in the northern Oremindi, whose geography is organized around three goat breeds and the inter-valley routes the goats navigated first.
- The vel-narcoth — "the ways the goats go" — is the route network through cliff faces and rock chimneys connecting the six valleys; its full knowledge is held by the route-keeper elder and cannot be administered from outside because no single point controls the full network.
- The goats' near-black coloration, pale markings, shaped horns, and pale eyes are altitude adaptations, not symbols; the elaborate ritual care structures are practical knowledge in traditional form, not worship.
- The "Devil Goat Worshippers" slur is wrong in every particular, and the Narcoshi have found that outsiders who reach this reading from one glance do not reliably revise it when given accurate information.
- Eastern-valley cheese commands premium prices at Iberos coast markets for flavor irreproducible outside conditions; the Narcoshi do not export the method.
- The Narcoshi are difficult to offend and difficult to impress, dry in humor, and direct in address, finding these traits natural for people whose primary knowledge is practical and technical.
